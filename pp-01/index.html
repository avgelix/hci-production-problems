The Reasons Why These Two Fonts Will Love Each Other

I promise there's plenty.

The original pairing from the typeconnection website suggested that Universe (a sans-serif font from 1957) paired well with Linotype centennial (1986), a neoclassical serif released by Adobe in honor of the 100th birthday of the Linotype machine.

In the same way, Raleway and PT-Serif, my chosen fonts, pair well because of the way their differences harmonize. The rigidity and simplicity of Raleway get along well with PT-Serif's rich history and elegance.

Similarly to Linotype Centennial, the font PT Serif is a serif typeface with humanistic terminals. It was designed for use together with PT Sans, and is harmonized across metrics, proportions, weights and design.

Raleway is a neo-grotesque sans-serif font, sharing these two properties with the font Universe. Additionally, Raleway is a display face and it features both old style and lining numerals, standard and discretionary ligatures, a pretty complete set of diacritics, as well as a stylistic alternate inspired by geometric sans-serif typefaces.
